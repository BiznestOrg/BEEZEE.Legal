3. Why We Collect Your Data (Legal Basis)

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), we process your personal data based on the following legal grounds:

Contract performance (Article 6(1)(b)) Most of the data we collect is necessary to provide the workforce management service you or your employer signed up for. This includes: account data, authentication data, work activity data, task data, scheduled shifts, inventory requests, and your language preference.

Legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c)) Shift and break records are also processed to comply with labour-law requirements for working-time record-keeping.

Legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f)) We rely on legitimate interest for: the audit trail (security and accountability), push notifications (timely operational communication), task photos (verifying work completion), task comments (operational feedback), and aggregated statistics (business management). You may object to processing based on legitimate interest — see Section 7 below.

7. Your Rights

Under GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

Right of access (Article 15) You can view your own profile, shifts, tasks, and notifications through the app. You may also request a complete copy of all personal data we hold about you by contacting us.

Right to rectification (Article 16) You can update your profile information and language preference in the app. Managers can correct task and shift data. For other corrections, contact us.

Right to erasure — "right to be forgotten" (Article 17) You can request deletion of your account and all associated personal data by contacting us. Note that some data may need to be retained to comply with legal obligations. In particular, Polish labour law (Kodeks pracy, Art. 94⁹ᵃ) requires employers to keep work-time records for 10 years after the employment relationship ends. If this applies, shift and break data cannot be deleted until the retention period has elapsed. We will inform you if any of your data falls under such an obligation.

Right to restrict processing (Article 18) You can request that we limit how we use your data. Account deactivation effectively restricts processing by preventing further access.

Right to data portability (Article 20) You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format. Contact us to request a data export.

Right to object (Article 21) You may object to processing based on legitimate interest (audit trail, notifications, photos, statistics). Contact us and we will assess your request.

Right to lodge a complaint If you believe your data protection rights have been violated, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the President of the Personal Data Protection Office (UODO — Urząd Ochrony Danych Osobowych), ul. Stawki 2, 00-193 Warsaw, Poland, or with the supervisory authority in your country of residence.

8. Security Measures

We take the security of your personal data seriously. The following measures are in place:

Encryption - All communication between the app and our servers uses HTTPS (TLS 1.2 or higher) - Data stored in our database is encrypted at rest (Azure SQL Transparent Data Encryption) - Uploaded photos are encrypted at rest (Azure Storage Service Encryption) - Data stored on your mobile device is protected by platform-level encryption (iOS Keychain, Android EncryptedSharedPreferences)

Authentication and access control - Your password is never stored in plain text — it is hashed using PBKDF2-SHA256 - Access tokens are short-lived (24 hours, configurable) and signed with HMAC-SHA256 - Refresh tokens are cryptographically random, valid for 7 days, and rotated on each use (the old token is invalidated when a new one is issued) - Role-based access control ensures that employees can only see their own data, while managers can see data for their business only - All data queries are automatically scoped to your business — one business cannot see another's data

Operational security - All secrets (database credentials, signing keys, API keys) are stored in Azure Key Vault — never in source code - Every data record includes an audit trail of who created and last modified it - Stale device tokens are automatically cleaned up when the push notification service rejects them - Logging out clears all local data on your device, unregisters your push token, and invalidates your server-side refresh token

9. Local Storage on Your Device

The BEEZEE mobile app stores data locally on your device using secure device storage (not browser cookies). This includes:

  • Authentication tokens — to keep you logged in
  • User profile — your name, email, role, and business association
  • Active shift and break state — to show your current status
  • Language setting — your preferred display language
  • Photo cache — recently viewed task photos for faster loading
  • Offline request queue — actions taken while offline, synced when connectivity returns

All local data is cleared when you log out. The app does not use any third-party tracking cookies, analytics SDKs, or advertising identifiers.
